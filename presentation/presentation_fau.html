SPH: Introduction

Ideas

  • Continuum domain is discretized into constant mass particles
  • Particles act as interpolation points as well
  • Not tracer particles - SPH is Lagrangian
  • Conceived as moving clumps/distribution of material
  • Characterized by field variables (dynamic and kinematic)
  • Associated kernel function (like shape functions in FEM) with a support radius
\[ A(r)= \int_\Omega A(r') W(|r-r'|)dr' \]
Approximation: \[ A(\mathbf{r}) \approx \sum _b m_b \frac{A_b}{\rho_b}W(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{r}_b,h) \]
Normalization: $\int W(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}',h)d\mathbf{r}' =1$, Compact support: $ W(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}',h) = 0 \textrm{ for } |\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}'| > r_c ,$ Positivity: $W(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}')\geq 0 $
Derivatives: \[ \nabla A(r)= \int_\Omega A(r') \nabla W(|r-r'|)dr' \]

Consistency: as $h\rightarrow 0$, $ \lim_{h\rightarrow 0 } W(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}',h)=\delta(\mathbf{r}-\mathbf{r}')$
Monotone decreasing, even and sufficiently smooth.

Derivatives

\[ \frac{\partial A}{\partial r } = \frac{1}{\Phi}\left( \frac{\partial (\Phi A)}{\partial x } - A\frac{\partial \Phi}{\partial x}\right), \] which takes the following forms for different values of $\Phi$. \[ \left(\frac{\partial A}{\partial r }\right)_{a} = \sum_b \frac{m_b}{\rho_b} \left(A_b - A_a\right) \frac{\partial W_{ab}}{\partial r_a} \text{ For } \Phi =1 \] \[ \left(\frac{\partial A}{\partial r }\right)_{a} = \frac{1}{\rho_a}\sum_b m_b \left(A_b - A_a\right) \frac{\partial W_{ab}}{\partial r_a} \text{For } \Phi =\rho \]
\[ \left(\frac{\partial^2 A}{\partial x^i \partial x^j} \right)_a = \sum \limits_b \frac{m_b }{\rho_b} \left( 4 \frac{\Delta x^i \Delta x^j}{\Delta r^2}-\delta ^{ij}\right)(A_a -A_b) F_{ab} \]

Governing Equations

$$ \frac{1}{\rho}\frac{d \rho}{dt} + \nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} = 0, $$
$$ \frac{d \mathbf{u}}{dt} = \frac{1}{\rho}\left(-\nabla p + \nabla \cdot \left(2 \mu \mathbf{D} \right ) + \mathbf{f}^B\right) $$ $$\mathbf{D} = (\nabla\mathbf{u}+\nabla\mathbf{u}^T)/2$$

Incompressibility model

Pressure and density relation for closure: $$ p = \frac{\rho_0 c_0^2}{\gamma} \left(\left(\frac{\rho}{\rho_0}\right)^\gamma - 1\right ), $$

Following grid based methods - Projection method: $$ \nabla \cdot\left( \frac{\nabla p}{\rho}\right) = -\frac{\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} }{\Delta t}, $$

Algorithm

While $t \leq \text{ end time}$:
  • $\mathbf{u}^* \gets \mathbf{u}^{n} + \frac{\Delta t}{\rho}\left(\nabla\cdot(\mu \mathbf{D}^n) + \mathbf{f}^B \right) $
  • $p \gets $ soln. of $\nabla\cdot \left(\nabla p / \rho\right) = \nabla \cdot\mathbf{u}^* /\Delta t$ ;
  • $\mathbf{u}^{n+1} \gets \mathbf{u}^{*} - \Delta t (\nabla p/\rho)$ ;
  • $\mathbf{r}^{n+1} \gets \mathbf{r}^{n} + \Delta t \mathbf{u}^{n+1}$;
  • $t \gets t+\Delta t$; $n\gets n+1$;

Free Surface Detection

  • Reflect unit vector about particle $a$
  • Check with all neighboring $b$ if included angle is $<\pi/4$
  • At least one reflected vector has no adjacent vector, mark it as free surface

Volume Conservation in ISPH

  • Divergence-free velocity is the condition for incompressibility
  • When finite time step updates are used, conservation may not be satisfied. \[ \frac{1}{\rho}\frac{d\rho}{dt} = -\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} \] \[ \frac{1}{\rho}\frac{\Delta \rho}{\Delta t} \neq -\nabla \cdot \mathbf{u} \]
  • Can we achieve better volume conservation while retaining simplicity of first order time updates?

Volume Loss

For explicit Euler time step, \[ \mathbf{r}^{n+1} = \mathbf{r} + \Delta t \mathbf{u}_{DF}. \] The corresponding gradient of this deformation can be written as, \begin{align*} \mathbf{F}^{n+1} &= \mathbf{I} + \Delta t \nabla \mathbf{u}_{DF}. \end{align*} \begin{align*} \det(\mathbf{I} + \Delta t \nabla \mathbf{u}_{DF}) &= 1 \end{align*} Using Cayley-Hamilton theorem, \[ \underbrace{\Delta t I_1 + \Delta t^2 I_2}_{\text{for 2D}} + \Delta t^3 I_3 = 0. \] $I_1$ corresponds to zero divergence: \[ I_1 = \text{tr}\nabla \mathbf{u}_{DF} = \nabla \cdot \mathbf{u}_{DF} = 0, \nonumber \] $I_2$, $I_3$ would vanish only for a vanishing $\Delta t$.

Volume loss - Demonstration

At time $t = m\Delta t$, the area ratio: \[ A(t)/A(0) = \left( 1 + \Delta t^2 I_2\right)^{m}\]
$\mathbf{u}_{2D} = -100x\hat{\mathbf{i}} + 100y\hat{\mathbf{j}} $ $ \mathbf{u}_{3D} = 100x\hat{\mathbf{i}} - 50y\hat{\mathbf{j}} - 50z\hat{\mathbf{k}}$
For Predictor Corrector updates: \[ A(t)/A(0) = \left(1+ \Delta t G_1 + \Delta t^2 G_2 \right)^m, \] $G_1$ and $G_2$: invariants of $\nabla\mathbf{u}_{DF}^n + \Delta t (\nabla \mathbf{u}_{DF}^n)^2/2$.

Proposed Pressure Equation

Let $\tilde{\mathbf{u}} = \Delta t \nabla p/\rho$ be the component of velocity that projects onto a divergence free field at every time step. Solve for $p$, using the condition $ \det(\mathbf{I} + \Delta t \nabla \mathbf{u}) = 1$: \begin{align*} 1 &= \det\left((\mathbf{I} +\Delta t \nabla \mathbf{u^*} ) +\Delta t \nabla \tilde{\mathbf{u}}\right) \\ &= \det\left( \mathbf{A} + \frac{\Delta t^2}{\rho} \nabla (\nabla p)\right)\\ &= \det\mathbf{A} +\text{cof}(\mathbf{A}):\frac{\Delta t^2}{\rho}\nabla(\nabla p) + \mathcal{O}(\Delta t^4) \end{align*} where, $\mathbf{A} = \mathbf{I} +\Delta t \nabla \mathbf{u}^*$ For a 2D domain: \[ 1-\det\mathbf{A} = \frac{\Delta t ^2}{\rho}\left( a_{22} \frac{\partial^2 p}{\partial x ^2} + a_{11}\frac{\partial^2 p }{\partial y^2} - (a_{12} + a_{21} ) \frac{\partial ^2 p}{\partial x \partial y }\right). \]

Elasticity

  • SPH is widely used in Elastic dynamic problems involving impact, fracture and plasticity.
  • Tensile instability.
  • Corrections/fixes to this instability over-damps.
  • The code uses elasticity models available in literature with Weakly compressible and Incompressible material models.
Assuming Hooke's law with shear modulus $\mu$, the rate of change of $S^{ij}$ is given by:
\begin{equation*} \frac{DS^{ij}}{Dt}= 2\mu\left(\dot{\epsilon}^{ij} -\frac{1}{3}\delta^{ij}\dot{\epsilon}^{ij}\right)+S^{ik}\Omega^{jk} + \Omega^{ik}S^{kj} \end{equation*} \begin{equation*} \dot{\epsilon}^{ij} = \frac{1}{2}\left( \frac{\partial v^i}{\partial x^j} + \frac{\partial v^j}{\partial x^i}\right) \quad \text{and} \quad \Omega^{ij} = \frac{1}{2}\left( \frac{\partial v^i}{\partial x^j} - \frac{\partial v^j}{\partial x^i} \right) \end{equation*} \begin{equation*} \frac{Dv^i_a}{Dt}= \sum_b m_b\left( \frac{\sigma_a^{ij}}{\rho_a^2} + \frac{\sigma_b^{ij}}{\rho_b^2} + R_{ab}^{ij}f^n \right)\frac{\partial W_{ab}}{\partial x_a^j} +g^i \end{equation*} \begin{equation*} \bar{R}_a^{xx} = -\epsilon \frac{\bar{\sigma}_a^{xx}}{\rho^2} \quad \text{when} \quad \bar{\sigma}_a^{xx}>0 \end{equation*}
